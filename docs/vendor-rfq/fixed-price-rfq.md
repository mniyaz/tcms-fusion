# Fixed Price (Prefix Price) RFQs

When you've already agreed on a rate with vendors or want to offer a preset amount, you can create an RFQ with a **fixed price (prefix price)**. Vendors can then **accept** the amount instead of quoting their own.

---

## When to use fixed price

- You have a standing rate with certain vendors.
- You want to offer a set price and only need vendors to confirm they can do the job.
- You're sending the same job to multiple vendors and want to compare availability (truck, driver) at one price.

---

## Creating a fixed price RFQ

1. In **Create RFQ**, select a booking and set the expiry date.
2. In the **Fixed price (RM)** field, enter the amount (e.g. `1250.00`).
3. Click **Create RFQ**.

The system creates the RFQ with that price locked. Vendors will see it and cannot change it.

---

## What vendors see

- The page title shows **"Vendor Quote – Accept fixed price"**.
- The fixed amount appears clearly (e.g. "Fixed price: RM 1,250 — Accept and provide truck & driver details").
- The **Quoted amount** field is read-only and shows the fixed price.
- Vendors fill in **truck**, **tonnage**, **driver name**, and **driver phone**, then click **Accept & Submit**.

---

## WhatsApp message

When you use **Send to selected vendors**, the WhatsApp message includes:

- **Fixed price: RM X,XXX**
- *(Vendor accepts this price – no quote amount needed)*

---

## RFQ Dashboard badges

On the **Active RFQs** list:

- **Fixed: RM X,XXX** — Indicates this RFQ has a fixed price; vendors can only accept.
- **Awaiting quotes** — Shown when the RFQ is open and no quotes have been submitted yet.

---

## Accepting a fixed price quote

The flow is the same as for normal quotes: expand the RFQ, review the vendor's truck and driver, click **Accept**, then go to Quick Assign to complete the assignment. The agreed price will be the fixed amount you set.
